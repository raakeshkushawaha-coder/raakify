What is a QR Code Generator?

+

A QR code generator is a free online tool that helps you create custom QR codes in seconds. QR codes (Quick Response codes) are two-dimensional barcodes that store information like website URLs, contact details, WiFi passwords, and text messages. When someone scans the QR code with their smartphone camera, they instantly access the information you've encoded.

What Types of QR Codes Can I Create?

+

Raakify.com supports six different types of QR codes, each designed for specific use cases:

1. URL QR Code

The most popular type of QR code. Perfect for sharing website links, landing pages, online menus, or any web address. When scanned, the QR code opens the URL directly in the user's browser. Ideal for marketing materials, business cards, product packaging, and promotional campaigns.

2. WiFi QR Code

Share your WiFi network instantly without typing long passwords. Simply enter your network name (SSID), password, and encryption type. When guests scan the code, their phone automatically connects to your WiFi network. Perfect for offices, cafes, hotels, restaurants, and home use. No more spelling out complicated passwords!

3. Text QR Code

Encode any plain text message up to 500 characters. Great for sharing instructions, product information, event details, or simple messages. When scanned, the text appears directly on the user's screen. Commonly used for educational materials, museum exhibits, product labels, and information boards.

4. Phone Number QR Code

Make it easy for people to call you with a single scan. The QR code stores your phone number with country code. When scanned, it opens the phone dialer with your number pre-filled, ready to call. Perfect for business cards, advertisements, customer service materials, and contact information sharing.

5. Email QR Code

Share your email address along with a pre-filled subject line and message. When someone scans the code, their email app opens with all details already filled in. This saves time and reduces errors when collecting feedback, receiving inquiries, or encouraging contact. Great for support materials, feedback forms, and business communications.

6. SMS QR Code

Enable instant text messaging with pre-filled message content. When scanned, the SMS app opens with your phone number and message ready to send. Perfect for quick responses, contest entries, subscription opt-ins, and customer engagement campaigns.

What Are the Best Practices for Creating QR Codes?

+

Follow these tips to create QR codes that work perfectly every time:

  • Test Before Printing: Always scan your QR code with multiple devices before printing to ensure it works correctly.
  • Maintain Contrast: Use dark colors for the QR code on a light background for best scanning results.
  • Size Matters: For printed materials, make sure the QR code is at least 2 x 2 cm (0.8 x 0.8 inches).
  • Keep It Simple: Shorter URLs and less data create simpler QR codes that scan more reliably.
  • Add Instructions: Include text like "Scan to visit website" or "Scan for WiFi access" near your QR code.
  • Logo Size: Keep logos small (15% or less) to ensure the QR code remains scannable.
  • Error Correction: Use high error correction if you're adding a logo or if the code might get damaged.
  • File Format: Use PNG or SVG for digital use, and PDF or high-resolution PNG for printing.
  • Placement: Place QR codes where they're easy to scan - at eye level and in well-lit areas.
  • Update Links: If you're using URL QR codes, make sure the destination link stays active and relevant.

Do QR Codes Expire?

+

No, static QR codes (like the ones created with our tool) never expire. Once generated, they work forever. However, if you're linking to a URL, make sure that URL remains active. The QR code itself doesn't expire, but it becomes useless if the linked content is removed.

Can I Edit a QR Code After Creating It?

+

Static QR codes cannot be edited once created. If you need to change the information, you'll need to generate a new QR code. This is why it's important to test your QR codes before printing them on permanent materials.

What's the Difference Between PNG, JPG, SVG, and PDF Formats?

+

PNG offers high quality with transparent backgrounds, perfect for digital use. JPG is compressed and smaller in file size, good for web use. SVG is vector-based and can be scaled infinitely without losing quality, ideal for large prints. PDF is great for professional documents and presentations.

How Do I Scan a QR Code?

+

Most modern smartphones (iPhone and Android) have built-in QR code scanning in their camera apps. Simply open your camera, point it at the QR code, and tap the notification that appears. Older phones might need a dedicated QR scanner app from the app store.

Can I Track How Many People Scan My QR Code?

+

Static QR codes (created with our free tool) don't have built-in tracking. If you need analytics, you can use a URL shortener service (like Bitly) for your URL QR codes, which provides click tracking and statistics.

Is It Safe to Scan QR Codes?

+

Generally yes, but always be cautious. Only scan QR codes from trusted sources. Your phone will usually show you the URL before opening it, giving you a chance to verify it's legitimate. Never scan codes from unknown sources or those placed suspiciously.

Why Isn't My QR Code Scanning?

+

Common issues include: QR code too small, poor lighting, damaged or low-quality print, insufficient contrast between code and background, or lens focusing problems. Try increasing the size, using higher error correction, or improving lighting conditions.
